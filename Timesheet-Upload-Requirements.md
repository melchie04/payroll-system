# Timesheet Upload Requirements

What a timesheet file must look like for the system to read it accurately.

---

## 1. The form

- Use the **standard STRON'L timesheet form** only. Other layouts will be rejected.
- Two versions exist and both are accepted:
  - **Days 1–15** (first half of the month)
  - **Days 16–31** (second half of the month)
- Use the version that matches the days being recorded. Do not write second-half dates on a first-half form.
- The printed form code in the bottom corner must be visible and not cut off.

## 2. One sheet, one person

- **One employee per sheet.** Do not combine multiple employees on one form or in one file.
- One employee may submit several sheets for the same pay period — for example, a period running the 12th to the 25th needs both the 1–15 sheet and the 16–31 sheet.
- Do not upload the same sheet twice. Duplicate days are rejected because they would be paid twice.

## 3. Fields that must be filled in

These are the fields the system depends on. If any is blank or unreadable, the sheet cannot be filed.

| Field | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| **Name of Employee** | Decides whose pay the hours count toward |
| **Period Covered** | Decides which payroll run picks up the sheet |
| **Client / Project** | Checked against the client selected at upload |
| **Daily IN and OUT times** | The hours themselves |

Write **Period Covered in full**, including the month and year — for example `June 1–15, 2026`. The printed date column only shows day numbers, so this line is the only thing identifying the month.

## 4. Signatures

A sheet should be signed by:

- The employee
- The supervisor (Checked by)
- The client representative (Approved by)

Unsigned sheets can still be uploaded, but they will be flagged and may be held back from approval.

## 5. Handwriting

- Use **dark blue or black ink**. Pencil and light-coloured ink read poorly.
- Write times in **24-hour format with a colon** — `07:30`, `13:00`, `17:00`.
- Keep each entry **inside its box**. Digits crossing the printed lines are often misread.
- Do not scribble over mistakes. Draw a single line through the error and write the correction clearly beside it.
- Leave unworked days **blank**. Do not write `0`, `—`, or `N/A`.

## 6. File format

| | Requirement |
|---|---|
| **Accepted formats** | PDF, JPG, PNG |
| **Preferred** | **Scanned PDF** — noticeably more accurate than a photo |
| **Maximum size** | 10 MB per file |
| **Scan resolution** | 300 DPI or higher |
| **Photo resolution** | At least 1600 pixels on the long edge |
| **Colour** | Colour or greyscale. Avoid pure black-and-white (1-bit) scans, which lose faint pen strokes |

## 7. If you must photograph the sheet

A scan is always better. If you photograph instead:

- Lay the sheet **flat** on a plain surface — no curl, no fold across the grid
- Shoot **straight down**, not at an angle
- Fill the frame with the sheet, but keep **all four corners visible**
- Use even light. Avoid direct glare, harsh shadow, and your own shadow across the page
- Only **one timesheet per photo**. Move other paperwork out of frame
- Keep hands, pens, and other objects out of the shot

## 8. Common reasons a file is rejected

- The sheet is not the standard form
- Part of the grid or the header is outside the frame
- The photo is blurred, skewed, or glared
- Period Covered is blank, incomplete, or unreadable
- Two or more documents appear in the same image
- The PDF is password-protected
- The file is over 10 MB

## 9. After upload

1. The system reads the sheet and matches it to an employee.
2. A reviewer confirms the employee, the period, and any values read with low confidence.
3. Totals are recalculated from the daily times and compared against the handwritten totals. Any disagreement is flagged.
4. Once approved, the daily hours are available to payroll.

Payroll collects **approved days only**. A sheet left unreviewed, or one covering the wrong dates, will simply be missing from the run — so submit sheets before the cut-off, and make sure Period Covered is correct.
